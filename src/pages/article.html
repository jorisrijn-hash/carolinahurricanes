Need to know:
the 2026–27 schedule

Eighty-four games, one more road trip than last year, and a January stretch that will decide whether the banner has company.

The schedule arrived on a Tuesday, which is how these things always arrive: quietly, in a spreadsheet, months before any of it matters.

Two extra games change more than the arithmetic. A longer season means a deeper rotation, more nights where the fourth line decides the result, and a bench that has to be built for attrition rather than for one perfect night in June. The club has spent the summer saying exactly that.

Opening night

Florida on 29 September, at home, with the banner going up first. There is no gentler way to start a title defence than against the team you spent four years measuring yourself against, and no harder one either.

Eighty-four games is not a longer season. It is a different sport.

The January problem

Six games in nine nights, all west of the Mississippi, ending with a back to back. Every season has a stretch like this and every season it looks harmless in July. It never is. Whatever the standings say on the first of February will have been decided in that fortnight.

The rest is familiar: divisional games stacked late, a light February, and a final week that could mean everything or nothing depending on what happens in the four months before it.
